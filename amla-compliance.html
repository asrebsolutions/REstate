"A professionally drafted Money and Terrorism Financing Prevention Program — ready to customise and submit."

Under R.A. 11521 (AMLA Amendment) and AMLC regulations, real estate developers are required to maintain a written Money Laundering and Terrorism Financing Prevention Program (MTPP). Failure to comply carries significant administrative and criminal penalties.

This template was adapted specifically for the Philippine real estate developer as a covered person under AMLC guidelines. It covers all required elements under the AMLC's framework for real estate covered persons — ready to customise with your company's details and submit.

Reference

Governing Laws and
Regulatory Framework

The following laws, regulations, and issuances govern anti-money laundering compliance obligations for real estate developers and brokers in the Philippines. This is a reference overview — not a legal opinion. Always consult qualified legal counsel for your specific compliance obligations.

R.A. 9160 as amended by R.A. 11521

Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA) and 2021 Amendment

The primary law governing AML compliance in the Philippines. R.A. 11521 (signed January 2021) significantly expanded the list of covered persons to include real estate developers and brokers for the first time, aligning the Philippines with FATF recommendations.

R.A. 10168

Terrorism Financing Prevention and Suppression Act

Governs the prevention and suppression of terrorism financing. Covered persons under AMLA are also required to implement terrorism financing prevention measures, including the screening of customers against OFAC, UN, and local watchlists.

AMLC Resolution No. 16, s. 2021

Implementing Rules for Real Estate Covered Persons

The AMLC's resolution specifying the obligations of real estate developers and brokers as covered persons, including registration requirements, CDD obligations, record-keeping, and STR/CTR reporting thresholds for real estate transactions.

AMLC 2018 Revised Implementing Rules and Regulations

IRR of the AMLA

Provides the detailed implementing rules for the AMLA, including definitions of covered transactions, suspicious transactions, and the obligations of covered persons in establishing and maintaining a compliance program.

FATF Recommendations

Financial Action Task Force — Recommendation 22

International standards on AML/CFT for Designated Non-Financial Businesses and Professions (DNFBPs), which include real estate agents and developers. The Philippines, as an FATF member, is obligated to implement these standards — which formed the basis of the R.A. 11521 amendments.

PD 957 / R.A. 4726 / R.A. 9646

Related Real Estate Legislation

The Subdivision and Condominium Buyers' Protection Act, the Condominium Act, and the Real Estate Service Act (RESA Law) — the foundational laws governing real estate practice in the Philippines, within which AMLA compliance obligations apply.

Compliance Tool

AMLA Compliance
Checklist for Developers

Use this checklist to assess your organisation's current compliance posture. Items checked indicate minimum requirements under AMLC regulations for real estate developers as covered persons.

Programme & Registration
  • Registered as a Covered Person with AMLC
  • Written MTPP document in place
  • Compliance Officer designated and reported to AMLC
  • MTPP approved by Board of Directors or equivalent
  • MTPP reviewed and updated at least annually
Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
  • Know-Your-Customer (KYC) procedures documented
  • Customer identification and verification process in place
  • Beneficial Ownership determination procedure
  • PEP (Politically Exposed Person) identification and screening
  • Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) for high-risk customers
  • Ongoing monitoring of customer relationships
Transaction Monitoring & Reporting
  • Covered Transaction Report (CTR) procedure in place
  • Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) procedure documented
  • Reporting timelines complied with (5 days for STR, within 5 days for CTR)
  • AMLC Compliance Reporting System (CRS) access established
  • Watchlist screening in place (UNSCR, AMLC lists)
Record-Keeping & Training
  • Customer records retained for minimum 5 years
  • Transaction records retained for minimum 5 years
  • AML/CFT training conducted for all relevant staff
  • Training records documented
  • Annual compliance audit or review conducted
  • AMLC examination readiness maintained
