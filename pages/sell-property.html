Thinking of Selling?
Let's Talk First.

Selling a property in the Philippines involves more steps than most people expect — especially when title transfer, capital gains tax, and buyer financing are involved. We assist sellers in understanding what to prepare, what to expect, and how to protect their interests through the process.

We work with individual sellers, families selling inherited property, and investors liquidating portfolio assets. Our role is to connect your property with qualified buyers and help coordinate the transaction from listing to closing.

Selling Inherited Property?

If the property was inherited, the title must first be transferred to the heirs' names before a clean sale can be completed. If this has not yet been done, please visit our Estate Settlement page first. We can assist with both the settlement and the subsequent sale.

Seller's Reference

Document Requirements
for Selling Property

The following documents are typically required to complete a property sale in the Philippines. Having these ready before listing will significantly speed up the transaction and give buyers confidence in your property.

Title & Property Documents
  • Owner's copy of Transfer Certificate of Title (TCT) or CCT
  • Tax Declaration (current, in seller's name)
  • Real Property Tax receipts (current year)
  • Tax Clearance Certificate from LGU
  • Survey plan or lot plan
  • Floor plan (for houses and condominiums)
Seller Identification & Authority
  • Valid government-issued ID of all registered owners
  • If selling through an SPA: notarised Special Power of Attorney
  • For corporations: Board Resolution authorising the sale, Secretary's Certificate
  • For inherited property: Extra-Judicial Settlement + BIR CAR already processed
Transaction Documents (at closing)
  • Notarised Deed of Absolute Sale
  • BIR Capital Gains Tax return (CGT) — seller's obligation
  • BIR Documentary Stamp Tax (DST) — typically shared
  • Certificate Authorizing Registration (CAR) from BIR
  • Transfer Tax payment to LGU
Seller's Tax Obligations
  • Capital Gains Tax: 6% of selling price or Fair Market Value (whichever is higher)
  • Documentary Stamp Tax: 1.5% of selling price or Fair Market Value
  • Withholding Tax (for corporate sellers or dealers in securities)
  • Income Tax (for those habitually selling property)

Tax obligations may vary. Always consult a licensed accountant or tax professional for your specific transaction.
